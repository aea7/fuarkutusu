# 10 Hidden Gems in London You Must Visit

London is famous for its iconic landmarks like Big Ben, the Tower of London, and Buckingham Palace. But beyond these well-known attractions lies a treasure trove of hidden gems waiting to be discovered.

## 1. God's Own Junkyard

Located in Walthamstow, this neon wonderland is a collection of vintage neon signs and electric art. It's a photographer's paradise and unlike anything else in London.

## 2. Little Venice

While Venice attracts millions of tourists, London's Little Venice remains relatively unknown. This peaceful canal area offers scenic boat rides and charming waterside cafes.

## 3. Leadenhall Market

Dating back to the 14th century, this covered market features stunning Victorian architecture and served as a filming location for Harry Potter's Diagon Alley.

## 4. Kyoto Garden

Hidden within Holland Park, this authentic Japanese garden features a tranquil pond, waterfall, and peacocks roaming freely. It's a peaceful escape from the city bustle.

## 5. Dennis Severs' House

Step back in time at this unique museum in Spitalfields. Each room is set as if the inhabitants just left, creating an immersive historical experience.

## 6. Sky Garden

While not exactly hidden, this free public garden atop the "Walkie Talkie" building offers stunning 360-degree views of London without the admission fees of the Shard.

## 7. St Dunstan in the East

This bombed-out church from WWII has been transformed into a public garden, where ivy and trees now grow through the Gothic arches.

## 8. Neal's Yard

Tucked away in Covent Garden, this small courtyard is splashed with vibrant colors and houses independent shops and healthy cafes.

## 9. The Viktor Wynd Museum of Curiosities

A bizarre collection of oddities, including shrunken heads, taxidermy, and other curiosities. Not for the faint-hearted!

## 10. Postman's Park

This small park contains the Memorial to Heroic Self Sacrifice, featuring ceramic plaques commemorating ordinary people who died saving others.

## Planning Your Visit

These hidden gems are scattered across London, so plan your route efficiently. Many are free to visit, making them budget-friendly alternatives to major attractions.

**Pro Tip**: Visit these spots on weekday mornings for the most peaceful experience and best photo opportunities.
